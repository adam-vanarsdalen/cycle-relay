# IVF Domain Knowledge — Cycle Relay

## The 14 IVF Cycle Stages

| Stage | Key Clinical Event | Coordinator Communication Focus |
|-------|-------------------|--------------------------------|
| Day 3 Baseline | AFC + baseline labs | Protocol start, injection training, expectations |
| Stim Day 3–5 | First response check | Confirmation response is normal, dose adjustments |
| Stim Day 6–8 | Mid-stim monitoring | Growth update, OHSS watch, next appointment |
| Stim Day 9+ | Approaching trigger | "Getting close," possible trigger tonight |
| Trigger Night | HCG/Lupron exact timing | Exact time, exact med, retrieval logistics — URGENT |
| Retrieval Pre | Day-of preparation | NPO reminder, arrival time, anesthesia prep |
| Retrieval Post | Egg count confirmed | Count, fertilization timeline, medication changes |
| Fertilization Report | Day 1 post-retrieval | Fertilized count, attrition is normal |
| Day 3 Embryo | Cell division check | Count, growth on track, heading to blast |
| Blastocyst Report | Day 5/6 final count | Blast count, grading, PGT-A, transfer planning |
| Transfer Day | Embryo transferred | Post-transfer restrictions, beta date |
| TWW Day 7 | Mid-wait progesterone | Lab OK, continued meds, beta countdown |
| Beta Positive | Pregnancy confirmed | Warm celebration, OB scan, continued meds |
| Beta Negative | Cycle failed | Grief-aware compassion, physician consult |

## Emotional Context Per Stage

**High anxiety stages**: Trigger Night, Fertilization Report, Blastocyst Report, Beta Negative, Beta Positive
**Moderate anxiety**: Day 3 Baseline, Stim Mid, Stim Late, Day 3 Embryo
**Guarded optimism**: Retrieval Post, Transfer Day, TWW Check-in
**Crisis communication**: Beta Negative (grief-aware, human, no clinical pivot immediately)

## What Must Always Be in Patient Messages
1. Acknowledgment of where the patient is in the journey
2. What is happening now (in accessible, non-jargon terms)
3. Clear next step (appointment, medication, timeline)
4. What to expect before the next contact
5. Who to call/message with questions
6. Warmth — this is the hardest thing most patients will go through

## What Must Never Appear
- Clinical opinions or diagnoses not authorized by the physician
- Prognosis statements ("Your chances of success are...")
- Medication changes not already confirmed by the physician
- Fabricated clinical data (never invent follicle counts, E2 values, etc.)
- Comparisons to population averages as reassurance without context
- Jargon without explanation (2PN, AFC, E2 — always explain briefly)

## HIPAA Communication Guidelines
- Patient portal is the primary HIPAA-compliant channel
- AI-generated drafts are reviewed by coordinator before sending — never autonomous AI-to-patient delivery
- No PHI stored by the AI tool — generation is stateless
- Coordinator documents communication in EHR after sending
- Phone communications require patient identity verification
- Email requires patient consent and should avoid detailed PHI without encryption

## Tone Guide Per Stage Group

**Monitoring phases (Stim Day 3–9+)**: Informative, grounding, matter-of-fact with warmth. Patients are anxious and counting. Acknowledge the numbers without catastrophizing or over-celebrating.

**Trigger Night**: Urgent, precise, warm but businesslike. Repeat the time. Make it impossible to miss. This message will be read in a state of adrenaline.

**Retrieval phases**: Compassionate realism. Attrition at every stage (eggs → fertilized → cleaved → blast) is normal and expected. Never frame losses as failures.

**Transfer Day**: Celebratory but grounded. Patients are superstitious. Provide clear, specific instructions. Celebrate the milestone while setting realistic expectations.

**TWW**: Compassion and grounding. The wait is terrible. Normalize the anxiety. Do not read into symptoms.

**Beta Negative**: This is grief. Treat it as grief. Space before pivot to next steps. Physician consultation must be explicitly scheduled.

**Beta Positive**: Warm joy, tempered for patients who've experienced loss before. Clear next steps. No overstatements about pregnancy outcome.
